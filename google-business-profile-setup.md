# Google Business Profile Setup — EB Services

Step-by-step guide to get EB Services showing up on Google Maps and local search results.

---

## 1. Create or Claim Your Profile

1. Go to **[business.google.com](https://business.google.com)** and sign in with a Google account (use one you'll keep long-term — this becomes the owner account).
2. Click **"Add your business"** or **"Manage now."**
3. Type **"EB Services"** — if it already appears (sometimes Google auto-generates listings), click it and choose **"Claim this business."** If not, select **"Add your business to Google."**
4. Enter the business name exactly as you want it shown: **EB Services**.
5. For the address, enter your actual service area address. If you go to customers (not a storefront), choose **"I deliver goods and services to my customers"** and then **hide your street address** — only your service area will show.
6. Set your **service area** — add the cities/towns you cover (e.g., your county or a radius of towns you'll drive to).

---

## 2. Choose the Right Categories

Categories directly control what searches you show up for. Get these right.

- **Primary category:** `General Contractor`
- **Secondary categories:** `Handyman Service`, `Bathroom Remodeler`

You can add more later (e.g., `Kitchen Remodeler`, `Home Improvement Store` if applicable), but start with these three. The primary category carries the most weight, so keep it as General Contractor.

**To set these:** Go to your profile > **Edit profile** > **Business category** > type the category name and select from the dropdown. Google has fixed categories — you pick from their list, not free-text.

---

## 3. Write Your Business Description

You get **750 characters.** Use them. Google reads this for relevance, and customers read it to decide whether to call.

**Keep it natural.** Don't stuff keywords. Write like you'd explain what you do to a neighbor.

**Example:**

> EB Services is a general contracting and handyman company serving [your area — e.g., "the greater Springfield area"]. We handle bathroom remodels, kitchen updates, drywall, tile, flooring, painting, and general home repairs. Whether it's a full renovation or a small fix, we show up on time and get the job done right. Licensed and insured. Free estimates.

**What to include:**
- **What you do** (remodels, repairs, specific trades)
- **Where you work** (city/region name — helps with local search)
- **What makes you different** (licensed, insured, free estimates, years of experience)

**What to avoid:** ALL CAPS, phone numbers (Google may flag it), "best contractor in town" claims, hashtags.

---

## 4. Set Your Business Hours

Set hours that reflect when you're **available to take calls and schedule work** — not necessarily when you're swinging a hammer.

**Recommended starting point:**
- **Monday–Friday:** 7:00 AM – 5:00 PM
- **Saturday:** 8:00 AM – 12:00 PM (or closed, your call)
- **Sunday:** Closed

You can adjust these anytime. If you take calls in the evening, extend the hours. The key is **don't set hours you won't answer the phone during** — missed calls during listed hours hurt your ranking.

Mark **holidays** as special hours when they come up (Google will prompt you).

---

## 5. Add Photos

**This matters more than you think.** Profiles with photos get 42% more direction requests and 35% more website clicks (Google's own data).

**What to upload:**

- **Finished work** (3–5 photos minimum) — clean bathroom remodel, tiled shower, new flooring, painted rooms. Good lighting, no clutter in frame.
- **Before/after pairs** — the single most convincing thing for a homeowner. Take the "before" from the same angle as the "after."
- **Active job sites** — shows you actually do the work. You at a job, your crew working, materials laid out.
- **Your truck/van** — especially if it has your logo or name on it. Builds recognition.
- **Tools/equipment** — a clean, organized setup signals professionalism.
- **You** — a headshot or a photo of you on-site. People hire people, not logos.

**Photo tips:**
- Use your phone in **landscape mode** (horizontal).
- **Natural light** beats flash every time.
- Don't use stock photos — Google may remove them and it looks fake.
- Add **2–3 photos per month** going forward. Google rewards active profiles.

**To upload:** Profile > **Photos** > **Add photos.** You can also add a logo and cover photo.

---

## 6. Get Verified (Postcard)

Google needs to confirm you're a real business at a real location.

1. After setting up your profile, Google will prompt you to **verify.**
2. The most common method is a **postcard mailed to your business address.** It takes **5–14 days.**
3. The postcard has a **5-digit verification code.**
4. When it arrives, go to **business.google.com** > click the banner or go to **"Get verified"** > **enter the code.**

**Important:**
- **Don't edit your business name or address** while waiting for the postcard — it can restart the process.
- If the postcard doesn't show up after 14 days, you can request another one.
- Some businesses get the option to verify by **phone or email** instead — take it if offered, it's faster.

**Your profile won't show up in search results until you verify.** Do this first and get it out of the way.

---

## 7. Ask for Reviews

Reviews are the #1 factor for local search ranking. You need them, and most happy customers will leave one — they just need a nudge.

**How to get your review link:**
1. Go to your Google Business Profile.
2. Click **"Ask for reviews"** (or find it under **"Get more reviews"**).
3. Copy the **short link** Google gives you. It goes directly to your review page.

**When to ask:** Right after you finish a job and the customer is happy. Don't wait a week — strike while it's fresh.

**Text message template you can copy/paste:**

> Hey [Name], thanks again for letting me handle the [job type] — glad it turned out great. If you have a minute, a Google review would really help my business out. Here's the link: [paste your review link]. Thanks!

**Tips:**
- **Text works better than email** — higher open rate, easier to tap the link.
- Don't offer discounts or payment for reviews — Google will flag and remove them.
- **Respond to every review** (good or bad) with a short, professional reply. Google tracks this and it looks good to potential customers.
- If you get a negative review, respond calmly, offer to make it right, and move on. Don't argue publicly.

## 8. Quick Wins (Do These After Verification)

### Add Your Services List
Go to **Edit profile** > **Services.** Add every service you offer as a line item. Examples:
- Bathroom Remodeling
- Kitchen Remodeling
- Drywall Repair
- Tile Installation
- Flooring Installation
- Interior/Exterior Painting
- General Home Repairs
- Deck Building/Repair

You can add prices or price ranges if you want, but it's optional. The service names themselves help you show up in searches.

### Make Google Posts
Think of these like mini social media updates, but they show up right on your profile in search results.

Go to your profile > **"Add update."**

**Post ideas:**
- Before/after photo of a recent job with a short caption
- "Now booking [month] — call for a free estimate"
- Seasonal services ("Getting your deck ready for summer? We're booking stain and repair jobs now.")

**Post every 1–2 weeks.** It takes 2 minutes and signals to Google that your profile is active.

### Answer the Q&A Section
Anyone can ask questions on your profile — and anyone can answer (even random people).

**Preload it yourself.** Go to your profile, ask common questions, and answer them:
- "Do you offer free estimates?" — "Yes, we offer free in-person estimates for all projects."
- "Are you licensed and insured?" — "Yes, fully licensed and insured."
- "What areas do you serve?" — "We serve [list your areas]."

This prevents wrong answers from strangers and gives customers quick info.

### Turn On Messaging (Optional)
Google lets customers message you directly from your profile. Only turn this on if you'll actually respond within a few hours. Slow responses hurt your profile.

---

## Maintenance Checklist (Ongoing)

- [ ] **Weekly:** Respond to any new reviews
- [ ] **Every 1–2 weeks:** Add a Google Post (job photo, seasonal update, etc.)
- [ ] **After every job:** Send the review request text
- [ ] **Monthly:** Add 2–3 new photos
- [ ] **Quarterly:** Check that hours, services, and description are still accurate
- [ ] **When something changes:** Update hours, phone number, service area immediately — stale info kills trust

---

## The Bottom Line

The whole setup takes about 30 minutes (minus the postcard wait). The ongoing maintenance is 10 minutes a week. For a solo contractor, this is the highest-ROI marketing you can do — it's free and it puts you in front of people who are actively searching for exactly what you do.

Get it set up, get verified, get 5 reviews, and you'll start showing up.
